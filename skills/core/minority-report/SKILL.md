# Minority Report

## Purpose

Capture the part of judgment that consensus would otherwise erase.

A minority report does not try to overturn the decision by force.
It preserves the unresolved concerns that may later prove decisive.

## Activate when

Use this skill when:
- a decision has been made but material objections remain
- one option was chosen over others under uncertainty
- the team wants a durable record of why dissent was not adopted
- governance or reviewability matters

## Do not use when

Skip this skill when:
- there was no meaningful disagreement
- the issue is trivial and fully reversible
- the objections have already been resolved in the final decision artifact

## Required sections

1. Decision taken
- What was chosen, by whom, and for what time horizon?

2. Unresolved objections
- What concerns remain alive even after the decision?

3. Discarded alternatives
- What credible alternative paths were not chosen?

4. Why not chosen now
- Why did the team defer or reject them at this moment?

5. Revisit triggers
- What future evidence or condition should reopen the decision?

6. Risk of being wrong
- If the minority view turns out correct, what will the cost be?

## Procedure

### Step 1 — State the chosen path
Describe the actual decision without spin.

### Step 2 — Identify the surviving dissent
Focus on objections that still matter after discussion, not every complaint raised during debate.

### Step 3 — Preserve credible alternatives
List alternatives strong enough that a future team could plausibly choose them.

### Step 4 — Add revisit triggers
Make revisiting conditional, not emotional. Name thresholds, dates, metrics, or events.

### Step 5 — State the downside of dismissal
Explain what would happen if the minority concern is ignored and later proves right.

## Output artifact

```markdown
## Minority Report

### Decision Taken
- ...

### Unresolved Objections
- ...

### Discarded Alternatives
- Alternative: ...
- Why it was not chosen now: ...

### Revisit Triggers
- If ... then reopen decision

### Risk if Minority View Is Correct
- ...

### Recommended Preservation
- Attach to: decision memo / planning doc / governance review
```

## Guardrails

- Do not turn the minority report into sabotage.
- Do not present weak or unserious objections as equal to strong ones.
- Do not omit the reason the majority chose otherwise.
- Do not preserve dissent without saying what evidence would settle it.

## Failure modes

Common failure modes:
- writing a complaint log instead of a judgment artifact
- failing to distinguish discarded alternatives from unresolved objections
- no concrete revisit trigger
- emotional language that hides the actual trade-off

## Escalation points

Escalate when:
- the minority concern involves safety, governance, or irreversible loss
- the chosen path depends on an assumption the minority explicitly contests
- no one can explain why the minority path was rejected now

## Completion condition

This skill is complete only when a future reader could understand both the decision and the strongest surviving reason to doubt it.
